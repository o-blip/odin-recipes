Mote con Huesillo

Description

As soon as the Spring starts in Chile, you can find little carts selling this traditional drink Wheat berries with dried peaches or Mote con Huesillos on the streets. A little like the Aguas Frescas in Mexico. It is very filling and a terrific snack if you are sightseeing. Should be served chilled

Ingredients

  • 12 small dried peaches
  • 1 or 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 4 cups of water
  • 3 cups cooked wheat berry or barley
  • 1 cup of sugar

Steps

  1. Soak the dried peaches on the water with the cinnamon and a piece of orange peel, optional, overnight. The next day, cook until soft, about 15-20 minutes after boiling.
  2. Place sugar in a small saucepan and cook over low heat until a dark caramel form.
  3. Pour over cooked dried peaches and stir to incorporate, this will give sweetness and color. You can do the same with molasses in the USA, dissolved in hot water from the cooked peaches
  4. Cook the wheat berry or barley
  5. Refrigerate
  6. To serve, place 1/2 cup wheat berries in a tall glass, add 2 peaches and cooking liquid to fill the glass.
  7. Add sugar if desired. Serve very cold.
